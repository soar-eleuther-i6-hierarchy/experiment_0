What the panels say

01 Reconstruction only ever bites at B0→B1

The share of edges that improve reconstruction collapses from 6–43% at B0→B1 to under 2% at B1→B2 and ≈0% at B2→B3 — at every layer. Deeper block pairs carry no reconstructive parent→child signal.

02 Frequency drives the deep pairs

92–99.9% of B1→B2 and B2→B3 edges are frequency-driven (coverage collapses on rare tokens) everywhere. What looks like deep hierarchy is co-occurrence between common features.

03 Density is not comparable across layers

B2→B3 candidate edges range from 431k (layer 12) to 4.7M (layer 6) — an order of magnitude. Raw coverage counts can't be compared layer-to-layer; only the recon/frequency-controlled view is stable.

04 B0→B1 recon signal is non-monotonic

The one place with real reconstruction signal (B0→B1) peaks early — 42.5% at layer 3 — dips to 6.3% at layer 6, and never recovers. Depth does not sharpen the hierarchy.

05 Qualitative coherence collapses at depth

Survivors spread over 5–7 distinct parents at layers 3–18 (each firing <20%), but at layer 24 they collapse to 2 parents — one feature (#14) firing on 41.9% of tokens with unrelated children. The rich metrics keep an artifact the labels reject: the qualitative test fails at L24.

06 A superparent leaked the AND-gate

Feature 14 escapes the superparent filter because the gate is AND(fan-out ≥ 30%, fires ≥ 10%): it clears firing by 4× (42%) but its fan-out is only 22%. Firing rate alone should disqualify it — superparent thresholds want per-layer calibration.
